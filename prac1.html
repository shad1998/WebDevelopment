Importance of Practice!

I like to think that the work of social science is to tell practice stories about an event or phenomenon. Practice stories explain something (how working class children end up in working class jobs, for example) by describing how it develops over time as norms, rules, and organizational arrangements are acted on and adapted by people as part of their daily lives, and in the context of their social lives (their communities, groups, networks, and families). Practice stories therefore help us to make sense of things as ongoing processes, both shaped by and shaping general patterns, arrangements, rules, norms, and other structures.
